Motive & Medium

Creative Vision

As an artist transitioning from traditional mediums into the digital art world, my creative process is deeply rooted in my experience with alcohol ink and pencil. These mediums allow me to explore fluidity and precision, bringing life to portraits and character drawings. Though I work with many tools, I am most drawn to classic pencil sketches of the human form.

My artistic style leans towards neotraditional, yet I’m on a personal journey to carve out something uniquely mine. Each piece I create is a reflection of my artistic growth—an evolving blend of what I know and what I’m learning.

Inspiration

I’m often inspired by the music I listen to and memories that resonate. Even a simple conversation can spark my imagination, pushing me to create, whether on canvas or screen.

Mediums

Some mediums have always resonated with me on a deeper level:

  • Alcohol Markers
  • Pencil
  • Ink
  • Watercolor

Process

My creative process begins with inspiration from daily life—music, memories, or conversations. From there, I break it down into these steps:

  1. Take note of daily inspirations
  2. Create a quick sketch of my concept
  3. Find reference photos for shadows and details
  4. Refine the drawing in a sketchbook
  5. Test colors and explore shading
  6. Choose a style to match the theme or mood
  7. Complete the piece, balancing texture, depth, and form
